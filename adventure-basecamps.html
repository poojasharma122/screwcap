Adventure Basecamps

These stays are built for the early risers, the trail wanderers, the mountain-road drivers, and the ones who pack walking boots before anything else. The Adventure Basecamps collection includes lodges, cabins, and comfort-first retreats that sit close to the land — and even closer to the action.

Featured Outdoor & Nature Stays

Each stay on this list offers more than just proximity to New Zealand’s best outdoor regions — they give you the space to return to, recharge in, and look out from.

Camp Glenorchy Eco Retreat – Glenorchy, Otago

Price Per Night: $270 NZD

  • Sustainable, design-led base at the edge of Mount Aspiring National Park
  • Short walk to rivers, tracks, and kayaking routes
  • Built with recycled materials and solar power

Camp Glenorchy doesn’t feel like a campground — it feels like a quiet corner of an alpine village. The energy is low-key, the design is intentional, and the views are standard issue South Island jaw-dropping. It’s the sort of place that supports your adventure, not competes with it.

Tongariro Crossing Lodge – National Park Village

Price Per Night: $285 NZD

  • Minutes from the Tongariro Alpine Crossing
  • Quiet lodge with hearty breakfasts and mountain views
  • Bookable shuttles and local guiding options

The basecamp for one of New Zealand’s most iconic hikes. Rooms are simple but comfortable, and the lodge offers everything you need before and after the long day out — hot showers, soft beds, and good advice on where to start.

More Info

The Lindis Pods – Ahuriri Valley, South Island

Price Per Night: $1150 NZD

  • High-country isolation with architectural flair
  • Floor-to-ceiling glass looking onto the Ahuriri River
  • Activities include fly fishing, hiking, and stargazing

The Lindis Pods offer a different take on the adventure stay — total seclusion, zero distraction, and panoramic access to one of the South Island’s most underexplored valleys. Perfect for solo travellers, couples, and slow adventurers.

More Info

Rainforest Retreat – Franz Josef, West Coast

Price Per Night: $365 NZD

  • Eco-lodges and tree houses in native bush
  • Walkable access to glacier tracks and thermal pools
  • On-site spa and communal spaces

For travellers heading up the West Coast, this is one of the most comfortable places to pause. The setting is dense rainforest, the vibe is social but peaceful, and you’re within easy reach of everything from heli-hikes to quiet bush trails.

More Info

Manapouri Lakeview Motor Inn – Fiordland

Price Per Night: $230 NZD

  • Affordable, clean base near Doubtful Sound
  • Direct lake views and easy access to kayaking and cruises
  • Good for early departures and late returns

Not every basecamp needs bells and whistles. This one keeps it simple — close to the water, friendly staff, and solid sleep between Fiordland adventures. It’s a practical launchpad for some of the country’s most remote scenery.

More Info

Riverside Matakana – Matakana, Auckland Region

Price Per Night: $410 NZD

  • Private villas near cycling trails and surf beaches
  • Great weekend base with outdoor gear storage
  • Wine, food, and nature all in easy reach

Matakana blends coastal energy with country calm, and Riverside’s villas sit right in the middle of it. Perfect for travellers who want to hike, surf, bike — and still return to a hot shower, a well-made bed, and a bottle picked up locally.

More Info

Lake Heron Station – Ashburton Lakes, Canterbury

Price Per Night: $325 NZD

  • Working high-country station with private accommodation
  • Access to tramping, fly fishing, and 4WD routes
  • Remote, no service, full reset

For travellers looking to disconnect completely, this is it. No screens, no service, just open landscapes and honest isolation. Lake Heron Station offers rustic comfort and a strong connection to the land it operates on.

More Info

Underhill Valley – Waikato

Price Per Night: $280 NZD

  • Off-grid hillside cabin with natural features
  • Close to glow worm caves, waterfalls, and biking trails
  • No Wi-Fi, no noise, no hurry

Built into the earth and overlooking a private pond, this stay isn’t about flash — it’s about pause. Great for solo adventurers, couples, or anyone needing stillness before or after the big activity days.
