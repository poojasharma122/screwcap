Relax & Recharge

Take a step back with these handpicked spa hotels, retreats, and peaceful lodges across New Zealand. From thermal springs to lakeside havens, each stay in this collection is designed to slow the pace, clear the mind, and offer a genuine reset.

Featured Relaxation & Wellness Stays

These are places where early mornings stretch out, phones stay tucked away, and the view does most of the talking. Expect natural surroundings, minimal noise, and hosts who understand the value of downtime.

Maruia River Retreat – Lewis Pass, South Island

Price Per Night: $495 NZD

  • Boutique eco-retreat surrounded by native forest
  • On-site yoga, spa treatments, and forest bathing
  • Farm-to-table dining using organic produce

Tucked away in the Southern Alps, Maruia River Retreat is made for quiet stays with intention. The villas are nestled in private bush settings, with a focus on natural healing and body-mind balance. It’s a strong choice for travellers seeking privacy with a touch of structure.

Book Now

Solitaire Lodge – Lake Tarawera, Rotorua

Price Per Night: $1150 NZD

  • Lakefront luxury lodge with geothermal access
  • Onsite chef, open bar, and guided nature experiences
  • Private hot tubs and panoramic decks

Located just outside Rotorua, Solitaire Lodge offers both luxury and seclusion. The views over Lake Tarawera are immediate and uninterrupted, with natural hot springs a short walk or boat ride away. Dining is included, and everything about the experience is calm but polished.

Book Now

Te Arai Links Lodges – Northland

Price Per Night: $980 NZD

  • Coastal escape with wellness pavilion and spa
  • Private villas with ocean views and fireplaces
  • Designed for slow living and nature immersion

Te Arai Links isn’t just for golfers — the lodges themselves are a full retreat into coastal stillness. The setting is remote but accessible, with a focus on high-comfort living and grounding experiences like massages, yoga, and ocean walks.

Book Now

Millbrook Resort – Arrowtown, Otago

Price Per Night: $440 NZD

  • Full-service resort with award-winning day spa
  • Mountain backdrop and open green space
  • Multiple dining options and heated pools

Close to Queenstown but calmer by nature, Millbrook offers space to unwind with everything onsite. From facials to hydrotherapy, the spa is world-class. The resort’s peaceful grounds and village-like design make it easy to do nothing at all.

Book Now

The Lost Spring – Whitianga, Coromandel

Price Per Night: $385 NZD

  • Geothermal spa pools and private soaking pavilions
  • Boutique suites available near the spa complex
  • Tropical gardens and massage therapies

This coastal hideaway combines natural thermal water with lush planting and tailored spa treatments. It’s not a resort in the traditional sense — the rooms are tucked nearby — but as a spa destination, it’s one of the Coromandel’s best-kept secrets.

Book Now

Split Apple Retreat – Abel Tasman, Nelson Region

Price Per Night: $1200 NZD

  • Exclusive retreat focused on wellness and nutrition
  • Infinity pool overlooking the bay
  • Art-filled interiors, gourmet plant-forward dining

Split Apple is ultra-private and fully tailored. With just a few suites, this health-oriented lodge focuses on full wellness — from food to breathwork to sleep quality. Located above the Tasman coast, it’s serene, scenic, and designed to shift your rhythm.

Book Now

Wai Ariki Hot Springs & Spa – Rotorua

Price Per Night: $360 NZD

  • Newly opened Māori-owned wellness destination
  • Based on traditional healing practices
  • Thermal mineral pools, steam rooms, and treatments

Wai Ariki is more than a spa — it’s a cultural experience built on deep-rooted knowledge. Guests move through a sequence of spaces, blending geothermal waters with modern design. There are boutique stays nearby to extend the visit into a full escape.

Book Now

Camp Glenorchy Eco Retreat – Glenorchy, Otago

Price Per Night: $980 NZD

  • Certified sustainable accommodation
  • Quiet village atmosphere with mountain backdrop
  • Meditation spaces, walking trails, and zero-distraction design

Camp Glenorchy proves that peaceful doesn’t have to mean expensive. Designed with sustainability and simplicity at the core, it’s a restful base near some of the South Island’s most awe-inspiring scenery. It’s not about pampering — it’s about presence.

Book Now
