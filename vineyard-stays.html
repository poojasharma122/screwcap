Vineyard Stays

Vineyard Stays in New Zealand

Discover accommodation in the heart of New Zealand’s wine regions. From Marlborough to Central Otago, these vineyard stays offer access to cellar doors, peaceful landscapes, and the kind of comfort that pairs well with a glass in hand.

Featured Vineyard Accommodation

Each of these handpicked stays sits among or beside the vines. Expect privacy, great views, and the added bonus of being close to the country’s best wine and food. These properties aren’t just near vineyards — they’re part of the experience.

The Marlborough Lodge – Marlborough

Price Per Night: $730 NZD

  • Luxury lodge on a private vineyard estate
  • On-site wine tastings and food pairings
  • Restaurant focused on regional produce

This restored historic property offers refined comfort at the heart of Marlborough’s wine trail. With only ten suites, expansive gardens, and full-service hospitality, it’s built for those who want to experience wine country with space and care. Vineyard access and local wines feature prominently in every stay

More Info

Craggy Range Vineyard Cottages – Hawke’s Bay

Price Per Night: $480 NZD

  • Private cottages on one of NZ’s top vineyards
  • Uninterrupted views of Te Mata Peak
  • Access to Craggy Range restaurant and cellar door

Set among the vines in the Tuki Tuki Valley, these architecturally designed cottages offer seclusion with immediate access to one of Hawke’s Bay’s most celebrated estates. Perfect for wine lovers looking for a quiet base and a short walk to their next glass.

More Info

Kinross Cottages – Gibbston Valley, Central Otago

Price Per Night: $350 NZD

  • Surrounded by vineyards and mountain views
  • Tastings from five boutique wine labels
  • Rustic-modern cottages with kitchenettes

Kinross is a favourite stop along the Gibbston wine trail. Each cottage is self-contained, peaceful, and just steps from cellar doors and walking tracks. You’ll find good coffee in the morning, Pinot Noir in the evening, and plenty of local flavour in between.

More Info

Black Barn Retreats – Havelock North

Price Per Night: $495 NZD

  • Collection of self-contained vineyard homes
  • Near local farmers’ market and Black Barn cellar door
  • Ideal for couples, families, or groups

Black Barn Retreats offers more than a place to sleep — these properties are tailored for travellers who want design, privacy, and access to Hawke’s Bay’s wine and food scene. Each stay is different, but all are surrounded by nature, vines, and great produce.

More Info

The Vintner’s Retreat – Marlborough

Price Per Night: $310 NZD

  • Villas with full kitchens and vineyard outlooks
  • Located on Rapaura Road, the region’s wine route
  • Pool, gardens, and outdoor areas for long stays

Set along the golden mile of Marlborough wine country, this relaxed accommodation offers flexibility and space for independent travellers. Walk to nearby cellar doors or stay in with a local bottle and vineyard sunset from the patio.

More Info

Burn Cottage Retreat – Cromwell, Central Otago

Price Per Night: $295 NZD

  • Eco cottages surrounded by trees and native plants
  • Short drive to Bannockburn vineyards
  • Adults-only, private, and peaceful

Burn Cottage offers a different take on vineyard accommodation — quiet, rural, and removed from the crowds. It’s a great choice for travellers wanting to explore Central Otago’s boutique wineries during the day and return to somewhere calm by night.

More Info

Te Koi – The Lodge at Bronte – Nelson

Price Per Night: $450 NZD

  • Coastal vineyard setting with estuary views
  • Private villas and suites in landscaped gardens
  • Seasonal dining with local wines

Nestled between sea and vines near Nelson, Te Koi combines vineyard tranquillity with subtle luxury. It’s a base for both wine region exploration and outdoor day trips, with a strong focus on quality and relaxation.

More Info

Hans Herzog Vineyard Cottage – Marlborough

Price Per Night: $420 NZD

  • One-bedroom cottage beside the vines
  • Direct access to organic winery and fine dining
  • Private garden, fireplace, and full kitchen

Hans Herzog’s cottage sits on-site at a working vineyard that’s deeply respected in Marlborough. It’s quiet, refined, and best enjoyed slowly — ideally after a long lunch and a cellar tasting just steps away.
